Water is everywhere, in the food you eat, the clothes you wear, and the energy that powers your devices. This hidden usage is called your water footprint.

Start by asking: What daily choices unknowingly consume thousands of litres of water?

  • Producing 1 kg of rice can consume over 2,500 litres of water!
  • A single cotton shirt? It takes 2,500 litres of water to make it.

‚úÖMake Small Changes


Reducing your water footprint doesn't require huge sacrifices. You can start with tiny, doable steps. The key is being mindful.


Think about what you eat, how you shower, what you wear. Each choice is a chance to do better.

  • Try Meatless Mondays swap out meat for lentils once a week
  • Keep showers under 5 minutes, every minute saved means 10-20 litres of water.
  • Wash full laundry loads and use cold water settings if possible
